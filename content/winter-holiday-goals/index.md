# New Projects ü§©

We're all more motivated to work on newer projects rather than debugging old ones. The new project I'd like to start is a simple polling app, which I'm going to call "Oponion"

It will be a way for people to make and share simple polls.

I have a *very rough* implementation of this made so far.

![Poll Demo](https://media.giphy.com/media/vOmyfldCvKPfAIFpfV/giphy.gif)

The styling is on different levels, *I know.*

But, if you can get the main concept of it, you can basically create polls with different options, titles, descriptions, etc.

I've only started working on this for the past hour or so.

Some cool ideas I have for this are adding cool data visualisation with [Charts.js](https://www.chartjs.org/docs/latest/charts/doughnut.html) w/ different colours.

I was wondering, should I add a login system to this? There's two sides to this and I think it's interesting to hear and understand both.

### No Login üôÖ‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è

I think many people don't want to create a whole account, or even choose a "Login With Google" if they just have to vote on a simple damn poll. It will make it less likely that people will actually use the site to vote on content, as they will have to do login drama over and over. This will lead to an incline in the amount of people who make these polls casually, because let's be honest, with a question like "what's your favourite colour?" nobody will bother to login and out a thousand times.

The main downside to this comes that users can vote again and again. This can unfortunately lead to some cheating in the polls which might draw away a good amount of people.

### Login To Vote üëç

This will make polls secure. People can't just randomly vote a thousand times on a poll to make it lean towards themselves. People will see a better use of this, and might start coming to using this app for larger and more serious polls.

The downside to this will definitely be the turnout of people who vote, which is a factor which might draw some away. If you require a login it will probably come as a side effect that less and less people will take part in the process and polls will be empty. This can draw away people who'll use the app for casual polls which nobody really cares to cheat on.

### My Thoughts üí≠

I'm leaning more towards not including a login at all. I don't want people to really use this for serious applications, and even if it was built in that manner I doubt many who make serious polls will actually use it.

If I make it as more of a casual site for polls, then I believe there will be a bigger turnout in the amount of people who partake in votes. In certain cases, I don't think anybody really cares on wining on *most* casual polls.

TDLR; I don't want to include a login system.
